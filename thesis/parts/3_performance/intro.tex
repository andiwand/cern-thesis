Providing good performance is the ultimate goal of reconstruction software, and it is crucial for the success of any experiment. First and foremost, the main focus is on physics performance. Deriving optimal measurements of physics objects is the primary motivation for reconstruction software. Computational performance is also important, as it determines the feasibility of running the reconstruction on a given hardware. Experiments with high track multiplicity must balance physics and computational performance to run reconstruction with available resources and achieve their physics goals. Track reconstruction algorithm performance directly impacts the quality of physics results obtained from the data.

In this part, the performance of the ACTS track reconstruction is presented and discussed. Chapter \ref{ch:perf/odd} presents an extended performance study with the OpenDataDetector (ODD) with a focus on track finding and fitting. Chapter \ref{ch:perf/itk} presents computational performance progress of the track finding algorithm after the ATLAS Phase-2 Upgrade with the new Inner Tracker (ITk). Physics performance is measured using efficiencies, fake rates, and resolutions and pulls of estimated track and vertex parameters. Computational performance is measured relative to reference configurations, since absolute numbers depend highly on the hardware.

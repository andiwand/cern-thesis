Providing good performance is the ultimate goal of reconstruction software, and it is crucial for the success of any experiment. First and foremost, the main focus is the physics performance. Deriving optimal measurements about physics objects is the motivation of reconstruction software in the first place. Potentially almost equally important is the compute performance, as it will decide over the feasibility of running the reconstruction on a given hardware in the first place. Experiments with high track multiplicity have to weigh off between physics and compute performance to be able to run the reconstruction with the available resources and achieve their physics goals at the same time. The performance of the track reconstruction algorithm directly impacts the quality of the physics results obtained from the data.

In this part, the performance of the Acts track reconstruction is presented and discussed. Chapter \ref{ch:perf/odd} presents an extended performance study with the OpenDataDetector (ODD) with a focus on track finding and fitting. Chapter \ref{ch:perf/itk} presents computational performance progress of the track finding algorithm for the ATLAS Phase-2 Upgade with the new inner tracking detector (ITk). The physics performance is measured with efficiency, fake rates, and resolutions and pulls of estimated parameters. The compute performance is measured relatively to reference configurations, as absolute numbers highly depend on the hardware.
